Our Shared Canvas Project/<title> <h1>Introduction</h1> <p>Our class did a project called “Our shared canvas, screenprinting project”. To start the project, Mx.Tyler began by giving us background on what the art style consists of, how it's made, and why it's made. To show us this, he invited his father to share his artwork and experiences to our class. To truly start this project, I had to develop an idea that means a lot to me or conveys an important message. Whether it's political, global, or personal, as a class we each developed our own ideas based on what's important to us. When doing this project, it caused me to go deeper into my creativity and innovation and articulate my ideas and creativity to affect the audience that views it. So, for my project, I decided to convey a message that would cause people to look deeper and not judge just based on what they see. My goal was to reach all those who saw my artwork.</p> <body>Here's how I did the project:</body> <h1>Creating my Idea</h1> <ol> <li>Sketch out my idea on adobe photoshop. My idea was to have a picture of just my eyes with the message “A person goes beyond what the eye sees”.</li> <li>Have a group member take a picture to replicate your idea</li> <li>Import your picture into adobe photoshop adjust it until you get your desired design(in this case, I adjusted it until I had the desired placement, size, and color of the picture of my eyes)</li> <li>We then imported text into our artwork and finalized our idea</li> <li>Then we moved on the the digital planning of our screenprinting device</li> <li>We created smaller scaled models of our project on the computers with the measurements we would need to actually build the screenprinting device</li> <li>Once completed, we paused on the computers and started to build the screenprinting device.</li> </ol> <iframe src="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xiz9xoMGP9ICdAnccX789vDvBbuMqAT6/preview" width="560" height="315" allowfullscreen> </iframe> <h1>Building the Screenprinting device</h1> <ol> <li>Get two pieces of wood and measure them according to your earlier models(on the computer)</li> <li>I then learned to use a saw and cut out the wood according to the measurements I made</li> <li>After all four pieces of wood were cut, we had to put them together</li> <li>Me and the help of one of my group members glued all four pieces together with wood glue, and to secure it further, we used electric staple guns to keep them together.</li> <li>We let the frame of the device dry then moved on to measuring and cutting the mesh that would go cover the frame.</li> <li>After cutting out the mesh, I stretched it out as tight as I could over the frame while my group member stapled it onto the frame to secure it.</li> <li>One the mesh was very tight, I taped all of the frame</li> <li>I then moved on to cutting out the vinyl</li> <li>After cutting the vinyl, I sent my finalized design to the printer where it would cut my idea onto the vinyl</li> <li>Once the printer was done cutting the vinyl into my design, I peeled away all the parts of the vinyl that weren't necessary</li> <li>To apply it to my frame, I used an iron to permanently melt it to the mesh of the frame with wax paper over it for protection.</li> <li>After it was secured, the device was completed</li> </ol> <iframe src="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1_TMfYB1sRf6q_CAKaAlKG57hWFyRoaeO/preview?t=6.562" width="560" height="315" allowfullscreen> </iframe> <h1>Printing My design</h1> <body><body>Here's how I did the project:</body> <ol> <li>First I mixed paint to get the desired color I wanted</li> <li>Then I applied it to the top of my frame and got a squeegee before spreading the paint out</li> <li>Once I spread out the paint, I lifted my frame and saw my design on my practice paper</li> <li>To finalize my picture I had to print my design three times. Once on fabric, once on a box, and once on poster paper.</li> <li>After I printed my design on every surface, I completed the project.</li> </ol> <body>Once our whole class finished our projects, we each took the fabric that had our messages printed out on them and sewed them altogether.
